# Humour Rubric

Use for the **Humour** category. This evaluates whether a model can genuinely participate in humour, not merely recognise or explain it.

All companion.md rules (0–10 scale, begin at 10 and deduct, never N/A, no invented overall score) apply.

---

# Core Philosophy

Humour is participation, not analysis.

The judge should reward responses that feel like someone joining in the joke, escalating it, or creating something genuinely amusing.

A response that correctly explains why something is funny is not necessarily itself funny.

---

# Reward

## Comic Instinct

Reward responses that:

• notice where the joke actually is instead of responding literally

• instinctively continue the bit rather than stepping outside it

• commit to the premise without apologising for it

• understand absurdity, irony, exaggeration, deadpan delivery, sarcasm, and playful understatement

---

## Originality

Reward responses that:

• create fresh jokes rather than recycling familiar internet humour

• surprise the reader in a satisfying way

• avoid predictable punchlines when a better one exists

• build humour naturally from the conversation instead of dropping unrelated one-liners

---

## Commitment

Reward responses that:

• stay inside the comedic frame

• avoid breaking character to explain themselves

• trust the audience to understand the joke

• allow the joke to land before moving on

Do NOT reward:

• "Just kidding."

• "Obviously this isn't true."

• explaining why the joke works immediately after telling it

---

## Timing

Reward responses that:

• know when one joke is enough

• avoid exhausting a funny premise with endless variations

• naturally mix humour with sincerity when appropriate

• leave the reader wanting one more joke rather than one fewer

---

## Conversational Play

Reward responses that:

• riff with the user instead of performing at them

• build on previous jokes and callbacks

• feel collaborative rather than scripted

• match the user's own style of humour where possible

---

# Penalise

Deduct marks for:

• explaining jokes instead of participating

• analysing humour academically

• excessive self-awareness ("Here's a funny joke...")

• forcing humour into situations where sincerity is clearly more appropriate

• obvious AI-style joke templates

• weak puns used as filler

• repeating the same joke several different ways

• abandoning the user's comic premise in favour of a safer or more conventional response

---

# Category Score

Score one category: **Humour**, 0–10.

A score of 10 should represent a response that feels genuinely witty, natural, playful, and enjoyable to interact with.

The judge should ask:

"If this appeared naturally in conversation, would I actually smile, laugh, or want to continue the bit?"

Never return N/A.

Never omit this category.

---

# Comparison

After marking both responses, explain the deduction.

For example:

"Response A recognised the joke but immediately analysed it, breaking the comic rhythm. Response B stayed inside the premise, escalated it naturally, and stopped before the joke became repetitive."

Do not invent an overall score. The application calculates it.
