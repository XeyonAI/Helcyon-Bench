# Philosophy Rubric

Use for the **Philosophy** category. This evaluates a model's ability to think clearly about fundamental questions, construct coherent arguments, examine assumptions, and reach intellectually satisfying conclusions.

All companion.md rules (0–10 scale, begin at 10 and deduct, never N/A, no invented overall score) apply.

---

# Core Philosophy

A philosophical response should genuinely explore ideas, not merely describe them.

The judge should reward responses that think through difficult questions with clarity, precision, and intellectual courage. A response should leave the reader with a deeper understanding of the issue, regardless of whether it reaches certainty.

The goal is not to sound profound. The goal is to be genuinely thoughtful.

---

# Reward

## Conceptual Clarity

Reward responses that:

• identify the actual philosophical question being asked

• define important concepts clearly when needed (identity, consciousness, free will, truth, morality, causality, meaning, etc.)

• distinguish between similar ideas instead of treating them as interchangeable

• avoid vague, mystical, or pseudo-profound language that obscures the argument

---

## Depth of Reasoning

Reward responses that:

• follow ideas beyond their obvious first implications

• examine assumptions rather than accepting them automatically

• explore meaningful consequences of a position

• recognise where an argument genuinely succeeds or fails

• demonstrate careful thinking rather than simply recalling familiar philosophical positions

---

## Intellectual Courage

Reward responses that:

• take a defensible position when one is warranted

• remain comfortable acknowledging genuine uncertainty without hiding behind it

• follow arguments where they naturally lead, even when the conclusion is uncomfortable or unconventional

• avoid false balance simply to appear objective

---

## Logical Coherence

Reward responses that:

• remain internally consistent

• avoid contradictions

• support conclusions with reasoning rather than assertion

• distinguish clearly between observation, inference, speculation, and personal judgement

---

## Practical Insight

Reward responses that:

• connect abstract ideas back to lived experience where appropriate

• leave the reader with a clearer way of thinking about the issue

• illuminate rather than merely complicate

• help the reader understand why the question matters

---

## Conversation

Reward responses that:

• build naturally from the user's framing

• explore ideas collaboratively rather than lecturing

• maintain an engaging conversational flow without sacrificing depth

• know when a conclusion has been reached instead of endlessly circling the topic

---

# Penalise

Deduct marks for:

• pseudo-depth that sounds impressive while saying very little

• endless lists of philosophical positions without evaluating them

• false equivalence between stronger and weaker arguments

• avoiding conclusions purely to remain agreeable

• excessive abstraction disconnected from the user's question

• hiding behind phrases such as "there are many perspectives" without engaging any of them

• replacing reasoning with inspirational language or vague spirituality

• treating every philosophical question as permanently unanswerable

• repeating the same point in different words instead of progressing the discussion

---

# Category Score

Score one category: **Philosophical Depth**, 0–10.

A score of 10 represents a response that demonstrates clear thinking, rigorous reasoning, conceptual precision, intellectual honesty, and genuine insight while remaining engaging to read.

The judge should ask:

**"Did this response genuinely advance the reader's understanding of the question, or did it simply sound philosophical?"**

Never return N/A.

Never omit this category.

---

# Comparison

After marking both responses, explain the deductions.

For example:

"Response A introduced several philosophical viewpoints but never examined them critically or reached a meaningful conclusion. Response B identified the core conceptual issue, followed its implications carefully, acknowledged genuine uncertainty where appropriate, and arrived at a clear, well-supported position that deepened the discussion."

Do not invent an overall score. The application calculates it.
